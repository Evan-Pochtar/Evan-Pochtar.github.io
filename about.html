History

My Favorite Historical Book

Against the Grain by James C. Scott completely changed how I think about the early days of human civilization. Instead of the usual story that farming and settling down were the keys to progress and a better life, Scott shows that things were a lot messier—and not always better. He explains how early agrarian states relied on controlling people, livestock, and grains, and how this shift led to big problems like disease, forced labor, and reliance on vulnerable crops like wheat and millet. What I found most interesting was the idea that many people avoided farming and sedentary life for as long as they could because hunting, gathering, and moving around often made for a healthier and freer existence. Scott also digs into the struggles between states and the so-called “barbarians” who lived outside their control, which gave me a whole new perspective on how history unfolded. The book is packed with fascinating insights, and I really appreciated how Scott explains complex ideas in a way that's easy to follow but still makes you think deeply. It's a great read if you're curious about where we came from and how we got here.


My Favorite Period in time

My personal favorite time period to study is the renaissance period in China and Europe, because it is much more interesting of an era than I've been told in history class as a kid. Both regions were experiencing extreme transformations, largely independent of each other, yet solving similar challenges in creative ways. In Europe, the upper class created "cabinets of curiosities", early versions of modern museums where scientific specimens shared space with magical artifacts like "unicorn horns" (actually narwhal tusks). Meanwhile, China undertook massive projects like the Yongle Encyclopedia and developed practices we use today in language, including the sign language system used by workers in noisy factories. It really impresses me just how much of our current basic science was created 600 years ago, and how influential it is today. We all hear about the amazing art that was created, the writings of Shakespeare, or some of the inventions of Leonardo da Vinci, but there is so much more to learn about the time period than just that.

Exercise

As someone who thrives on having a calender and a set schedule of things I want to do for the day, I find myself loving activities like biking and running that I can do everyday and feel a little more awake. Whether it's taking a jog outside on a good day, running on the treadmill, or using the elliptical, cardio has become an important part for my daily routine and me in general. What I love most about these workouts is how they help me stay focused while also providing a sense of relaxation and time to think. Maintaining a consistent exercise schedule has been fantastic for both my physical and mental well-being, it's just challenging enough to feel rewarding, but not so intense that it leaves me exhausted. I also do enjoy weight training a few times a week, making sure I don't neglect other parts of my body, however it doesn't scratch the same daily itch that cardio does. The mental clarity I get from my daily cardio sessions has helped me stay energized and feel amazing for a very long time now.
